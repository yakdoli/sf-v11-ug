Pareto Chart

This sample illustrates the Pareto chart type supported by Essential Chart.

A Pareto chart is a special type of bar chart where the values being plotted are arranged in descending order. The graph is accompanied by a line that shows the cumulative totals of each category, left to right.

Pareto Chart

Features:

  • Pareto Chart

    The chart control supports Pareto charts by using a combination of line and column charts with multiple axes.

    The left vertical axis is the frequency of occurrence, but it can alternatively represent cost or other important units of measure. The right vertical axis is the cumulative percentage of the total number of occurrences, total cost, or total of the particular unit of measure. Because the reasons are in decreasing order, the cumulative function is a concave function.

    Its purpose is to highlight the most important factor among a large set of factors. In quality control, the Pareto chart often represents the most common sources of defects, the highest occurring type of defect, or the most frequent reasons for customer complaints, etc. The Pareto chart is one of the seven basic tools of quality control.
